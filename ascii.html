glossary


    

      
algorithms — what they look like

      
flow fieldsmooth, drifting clouds. organic and fluid.

      
domain warpfolded, twisted, geological. like wood grain or bent smoke.

      
julia setcrystalline fractal edges. intricate and symmetric in unexpected ways.

      
reactionspots, stripes, or labyrinths. like animal skin or coral growth.

      
voronoicellular regions with visible boundaries. like cracked mud or a dragonfly wing.

      
wavesinterference rings and ripple patterns.

      
rorschachbilaterally mirrored — any pattern becomes an inkblot.

      
l-systembranching structures. ferns, trees, roots.

      
automatagrid-based growth patterns. can be organic, crystalline, or chaotic depending on the random ruleset.

      
cliffordwispy orbital trails. the density of millions of iterated points forms the image.

      
dlalightning bolts, snowflakes, coral. particles walk randomly and stick on contact.

    


    

      
layer + blend

      
layergenerates a second field with a different algorithm and overlays it on the first. none means no overlay.

      
blend modehow the two layers combine mathematically. add and screen lighten. multiply darkens. max and min take the stronger or weaker value at each point. difference creates edges where the two differ most.

    


    

      
charset — what the characters do

      
densitysmooth gradient from empty to full. the most faithful rendering of the underlying field.

      
hi-contrastonly five steps. very graphic, almost like a stamp or linocut.

      
gritcharacters picked for visual weight, not tonal order. the texture is rough regardless of the field underneath.

      
sparse / alpha / binary / symbol / punctdifferent character pools that change the surface feel. combine with contrast and density sliders to find the right register.

    


    

      
sliders

      
complexitymore iterations, more layers, more detail. higher values are slower to compute.

      
densitybiases the output toward sparse (mostly empty) or full (mostly filled).

      
contrastlow = milky and compressed. high = stark, mostly empty or full, few mid-tones.

      
symmetry0 = none. 1 = left-right mirror. 2 = also top-bottom. 3 = all four quadrants. 4 = adds rotational fold. higher values impose mandala-like structure on any algorithm.

      
zoomzooms into the generated field. high zoom on julia set or clifford reveals very different detail.

      
offset x / ypans the view when zoomed in. lets you explore a different region of the same seed.

    


    

      
other controls

      
↺ (next to generate)randomises everything at once — algorithm, layer, charset, and all sliders — then generates.

      
flow smearshifts characters slightly along the field's gradient direction. gives the image a sense of movement. on by default.

      
tile repeatmirrors the field into a 2x2 grid. any image becomes a seamless repeating pattern.

      
pin seedlocks the random seed so generate keeps the same underlying structure. useful for comparing how algorithm or charset changes transform the same form.
